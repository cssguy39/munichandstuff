Café Luitpold

When one writes about restaurants, it's usually about the food, ambiance, and service. If the combination is good, the place gets recommended. If not, you go somewhere else. But some cafés are beyond that, and Café Luitpold is one of them. Even if they start serving instant coffee and hot dogs, you'd still have to go. This is not to say you should expect to be dissappointed: the restaurant has been in the Michelin Guide for years and is famous for its pastry and pralines. But I'll leave the food and coffee for you to judge and serve a bit of history instead.

It's 1888. Prince-regent Luitpold is in power. Munich blooms. Later people would remember this period as the good old times. First image that the cafe looked like this: The location can hold 2000 people – astonishing number even for today. Several halls. Newspaper devotes a page to the grand opening calling the place a coffee palace.
