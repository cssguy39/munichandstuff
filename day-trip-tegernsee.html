Day Trip to Tegernsee

This is the mother of all day trips from Munich: you get all the beautiful things Bavaria has to offer on one day. Get your Instagram ready. If you're going with a friend who likes to be photographed, stay strong. (If you're lucky, the phone battery will die before you do).

Tegernsee is both the name of the lake and of one of the towns along the shore. It’s also a brand of beer. We’ll be passing by the brewery and the adjoining beer hall in the Tegernsee Castle. The pale lager beer, Tegernsee Hell, is especially popular.

Below is the must-see programme, followed by some extra ideas in case you’re going with a bike (which can be taken on the train) or have more time to explore.

During colder seasons, take an extra sweater: it might be cooler at the top. In summer, take some headgear and sunscreen — the sun can get cruel on longer walks.

The best time to go is during the week: you avoid the crowds, and all the shops are open (which might be a mixed blessing). As everywhere else in Bavaria, stores are shut on Sundays.
